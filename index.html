Information for Parents

Everything you need to know to support your wrestler

Is Wrestling Safe for Girls?

Wrestling is one of the safest contact sports when coached properly. Girls compete exclusively against other girls in their weight class, and our certified coaches prioritize safety above all else. Research shows wrestling has lower injury rates than many traditional girls' sports.
  • Lower injury rates than soccer, basketball, and cheerleading according to national safety data
  • All coaches are certified and trained in current safety protocols and injury prevention
  • Girls only compete against girls‚Äîno co-ed matches
  • Weight classes ensure fair and safe competition
  • Athletic trainers present at all practices and competitions
  • Comprehensive concussion protocols and monitoring
  • Emphasis on proper technique reduces injury risk significantly

Getting Started (No Experience Required!)

Most of our best wrestlers came in with zero experience. That's the beauty of wrestling‚Äîeveryone starts somewhere, and we're here to teach every step. Your daughter doesn't need to be the strongest or most athletic to succeed.
  • Absolutely no prior wrestling experience necessary‚Äîwe teach everything from scratch
  • Elementary program cost is only $10 per wrestler (includes t-shirt, pictures, certificate, treats)
  • Basic equipment needed: wrestling shoes, headgear, and comfortable athletic wear
  • Practice schedules designed to balance academics, social life, and athletics
  • Beginners practice separately until they're ready to compete
  • First tournaments are low-pressure and designed for learning
  • Remember to send a note to school if your child is staying for practice and not riding the bus home

Weight Management Done Right

We take a healthy, science-based approach to weight management. Cutting weight is NOT allowed or encouraged. We emphasize proper nutrition and competing at a healthy, natural weight.
  • NO weight cutting‚Äîathletes compete at healthy, natural weights
  • Mandatory hydration testing before weigh-ins
  • Nutritionist consultation available to all wrestlers
  • Focus on building strength and healthy eating habits, not losing weight
  • Regular health monitoring and body composition assessments
  • Parents involved in weight class selection and management plans
  • We follow WIAA and USA Wrestling guidelines strictly, for athlete health and safety

  • Women's wrestling is now an NCAA championship sport with the first tournament in 2026
  • Over 76 NCAA programs actively offering women's wrestling opportunities‚Äîand growing every year
  • Many programs are actively seeking female wrestlers and have scholarships available
  • College coaches frequently attend high school tournaments recruiting athletes
  • Growing international competition opportunities including World Championships
  • Life skills translate to success in any career path‚Äîwrestlers are known for their work ethic
  • Network of coaches and mentors to guide college recruitment process

How to Support Your Wrestler

Your support makes all the difference. Here's how to be your daughter's biggest champion while helping her develop independence and resilience.
  • Attend matches and tournaments to show your support‚Äîyour presence matters
  • Focus on effort and improvement, not just wins and losses‚Äîgrowth is the goal
  • Help maintain healthy eating habits and ensure adequate sleep for recovery
  • Celebrate small victories and learning moments, not just tournament wins
  • Let coaches handle technique and strategy‚Äîtrust the process
  • Volunteer at tournaments and team events to build community
  • Connect with other wrestling families for carpools, support, and friendship
  • After tough losses, listen first and encourage‚Äîevery champion has lost matches
  • Pick up your elementary wrestlers promptly after practice (4:30 PM for K-4, 5:00 PM for 5-6)
